Grilled Salmon

Description

Grilled salmon is a tasty and easy dish that brings out the best in this flavorful fish. Simply seasoned with salt, pepper, and maybe a squeeze of lemon, the salmon fillet is cooked on a hot grill, giving it a delicious smoky taste. The result is a perfect balance of crispy skin and tender, flaky fish. Whether you prefer a zesty marinade or keeping it plain, grilled salmon is a versatile and healthy choice for a delicious meal. Just add a squeeze of lemon, and you have a simple yet satisfying dish that's sure to please seafood lovers and those looking for a nutritious option.

Ingredients

  • 1 ½ pounds salmon fillets
  • lemon pepper to taste
  • garlic powder to taste
  • salt to taste
  • ⅓ cup soy sauce
  • ⅓ cup brown sugar
  • ⅓ cup water
  • ¼ cup vegetable oil

Steps

  1. Season salmon fillets with lemon pepper, garlic powder, and salt.
  2. Stir soy sauce, brown sugar, water, and vegetable oil together in a small bowl until sugar is dissolved. Place fish in a large resealable plastic bag; add soy sauce mixture, seal, and turn to coat. Refrigerate for at least 2 hours.
  3. Preheat an outdoor grill for medium heat and lightly oil the grate.
  4. Place salmon on the preheated grill, and discard marinade. Cook salmon until fish flakes easily with a fork, about 6 to 8 minutes per side.
